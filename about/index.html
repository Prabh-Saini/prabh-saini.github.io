Section 05 Shared working method REV 01

Observe / Plan / Experiment / Refine

One method, two ways of making.

Engineering and photography alternate throughout the process, but both depend on deliberate observation and repeated refinement.

CuriosityPatienceExperimentationCompositionIteration

Observe

Begin with the subject and the context before choosing a response.

Engineering

Understand the problem, constraints, stakeholders, and existing system.

Photography

Study the light, subject, environment, and possibility of a moment.

Define

Turn broad observations into a clear point of attention.

Engineering

Set requirements, assumptions, risks, and measurable success criteria.

Photography

Choose the story, visual tension, or relationship the frame should hold.

Plan

Give the idea enough structure to be tested without over-prescribing it.

Engineering

Create a model, drawing, architecture, or practical sequence of work.

Photography

Consider location, timing, perspective, movement, and available light.

Experiment

Use small tests to replace assumptions with evidence.

Engineering

Prototype the uncertain parts and test the highest-risk decisions first.

Photography

Change angle, distance, exposure, and timing to discover the strongest frame.

Build / Capture

Commit to a direction while staying alert to what the work reveals.

Engineering

Assemble the system with explicit interfaces and observable behaviour.

Photography

Capture with intention while remaining responsive to the real scene.

Review

Compare the result with the purpose rather than the first idea.

Engineering

Test behaviour, usability, reliability, performance, and edge cases.

Photography

Assess composition, focus, tonal balance, emotion, and narrative value.

Refine

Remove noise and improve the decisions that carry the experience.

Engineering

Simplify, optimise, document, and resolve the most meaningful weaknesses.

Photography

Edit with restraint, preserve natural detail, and strengthen visual direction.

Share

Present the work with enough context for another person to understand it.

Engineering

Explain the problem, trade-offs, outcome, and limitations clearly.

Photography

Sequence and caption the images so the story can continue beyond the frame.
